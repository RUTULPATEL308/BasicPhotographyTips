Classic Lighting Patterns

Rembrandt Lighting

Creates a triangle of light on the shadowed side of the face. Classic and dramatic portrait lighting.

Setup: Light at 45¬∞ angle, slightly above eye level

Butterfly Lighting

Creates a butterfly-shaped shadow under the nose. Flattering for most face shapes.

Setup: Light directly in front, above eye level

Split Lighting

Divides the face into equal halves of light and shadow. Creates dramatic, moody portraits.

Setup: Light at 90¬∞ to the side of the subject

Loop Lighting

Creates a small shadow loop from the nose. Natural and flattering for most subjects.

Setup: Light at 30-45¬∞ angle, eye level or slightly above

Pro Lighting Tips

01

Start Simple

Master one light before adding more. A single well-placed light can create stunning results.

02

Watch the Eyes

Ensure there's always light in your subject's eyes. This creates connection and life in portraits.

03

Control Shadows

Shadows are as important as highlights. Use fill light or reflectors to control contrast.

04

Practice Daily

Study light throughout the day. Notice how it changes and affects the mood of scenes.
